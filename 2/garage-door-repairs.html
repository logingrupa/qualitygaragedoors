What we fix

Common Sacramento garage door repairs.

Most calls we run are one of a short list of failures. We know them well, we carry the parts, and we resolve most of them in a single visit.

  • Broken torsion or extension springs — both replaced together, every time.
  • Snapped or frayed cables on either side of the door.
  • Doors off-track from impact, age, or worn rollers.
  • Worn rollers, hinges, or end bearings causing noise or binding.
  • Bent or damaged panels — section replacement when possible.
  • Opener trouble — gear assemblies, circuit boards, safety eye sensors, remotes.
  • Drum and shaft issues on heavier insulated doors.
  • Routine maintenance — full lube + tune + safety check.

What's included with every repair

Every repair we do — large or small — includes a complimentary lube, tune and full safety inspection. That's a $129 service on its own. Free with every service call.

  • Lubrication of all hinges, rollers, and bearings.
  • Spring tension and balance adjustment.
  • Hardware tightening — track bolts, brackets, opener arms.
  • Safety reverse and photo-eye sensor calibration.
  • Written 90-day labor warranty on the repair.

Why we replace both springs

Torsion springs are rated for a finite number of cycles. When one breaks, the other has been through the same wear. Replacing only the broken spring leaves an unbalanced door, strains the opener, and almost always means a second service call within a year. We replace both — every time.
