A / BROKEN SPRINGS

Broken springs.

Broken springs are the most common garage door repair. If your door won't budge — even when the release is pulled and you try to lift it manually — odds are you have a broken spring. Do not attempt to open or close the door; you'll only cause further damage.

Springs operate under extreme tension, which is what makes a properly balanced door feel light. Most people don't realize how heavy their door is until a spring breaks. Over time, daily use causes springs to become brittle.

We replace both springs as standard. They wear at the same rate, so replacing only the broken one puts excess load on the survivor and shortens its life.

Safety: never attempt spring work yourself. The tension stored in a torsion spring can cause serious injury. Call a licensed professional.

B / BROKEN CABLES

Broken cables.

Cables attach to the bottom of the door on each side and are part of the torsion system. They help lift the door and, because they're under tension, keep the door level. When a cable breaks, the door often ends up off track, sitting sideways in the opening.

Damage varies depending on where the door was when the cable broke. We use heavy-duty hardware cables to maximize lifespan.

C / OFF‑TRACK

Door off the track.

A door off track can happen for many reasons: a broken or faulty spring, broken cables, cables off the drum, the door binding on jambs, or a vehicle hit. Depending on whether the door is fully open, midway, or tilted, the repair scope varies.

We assess damage, determine cause, and repair safely. If your door has fallen off the tracks, call us today.
