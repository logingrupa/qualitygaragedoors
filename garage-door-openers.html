Opener Types We Service

There are many different brands and types of garage door openers. Understanding the differences helps you make the best choice for your home and lifestyle.

Belt Drive Recommended

The quietest and most reliable option. Belt drives use a rubber belt instead of a chain, resulting in smooth, near-silent operation. Just as strong and durable as chain drives — our top recommendation for most homes.

Chain Drive

The most common type. Chain drives are durable and affordable but tend to be noisier than belt drives. A good option for detached garages where noise isn't a concern.

Screw Drive

Uses a threaded steel rod to move the trolley. Requires significant maintenance and doesn't perform as well in areas with changing climates like Sacramento. Not our preferred recommendation.

Jackshaft Opener

Mounts to the wall beside the door and opens it by turning the torsion tube. Ideal for garages with limited ceiling space or high-lift door configurations.

Garage Door Opener Repairs

The garage door opener gets a lot of use. When something goes wrong, it's important to have an experienced technician diagnose and repair the specific type of opener you have. Common repairs include:

  • Gear & sprocket assembly — Motor runs but door won't move (most common failure)
  • Circuit board replacement — Remote pressed but nothing happens
  • Safety eye alignment — Door reverses or won't close
  • Trolley & rack repair — Grinding or jerky movement
  • Limit switch adjustment — Door doesn't open/close fully
  • Capacitor replacement — Motor hums but won't start
  • Remote programming — New remotes or keypad setup

There is nothing more inconvenient than a garage door that won't work — especially when it's cold and wet outside. Call us today to schedule your repair.
