Opener Repairs

If your existing opener is misbehaving — humming without lifting, reversing partway, ignoring the remote, or the lights flashing in patterns — we diagnose and repair on the same call. Common opener repair work includes:

  • Logic board replacement
  • Limit switch & travel calibration
  • Drive belt or chain replacement
  • Capacitor replacement
  • Safety sensor (photo eye) alignment and replacement
  • Remote and keypad pairing
  • Wall console replacement
  • Trolley and rail repair

When To Replace Instead

If your opener is over 15 years old, lacks a battery backup (now required by California for new installs), or has a logic board that's been "kind of working" for months — replacement is usually the better economics. We'll tell you the truth on which way to go before we touch the unit.

California rule All new garage door openers installed in California must include a functioning battery backup. We carry the compliant Liftmaster units on the truck.
